Privacy Policy (Datenschutzerklärung)

This website hosts FuBot, an experimental chatbot developed by researchers specialized in digital governance. The chatbot relies on several external service providers, including OpenAI and Amazon AWS. Its quality of responses is comparable to that generated by mainstream large language models. User privacy has always been a top priority in the development and application of the chatbot.

2. What data is processed?

When you use FuBot, the following information may be processed:

  • The passcode assigned to you for this study or service.
  • The questions you submit to the chatbot.
  • The answers the chatbot provides.
  • Timestamps and basic technical information required for operation and security.

To protect users' privacy, the following information will NOT be processed.

  • Device identifiers or device metadata
  • Server or access log files
  • IP addresses

3. Location of processing

The chatbot was developed and deployed in Germany. Hence, data processing also takes place in Germany. The processing of personal data on this website is carried out in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and applicable German data protection laws.

4. Purpose of processing

The data is processed for research and evaluation purposes in the field of digital governance, and to improve the functioning and reliability of the chatbot.

5. Legal basis

The legal basis for processing is your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) and, where applicable, the performance of a task carried out in the public interest or in the exercise of official authority (Art. 6(1)(e) GDPR).

6. Storage period

Chat data and passcodes are stored for the duration of the research project and may be retained in anonymised or aggregated form for scientific analysis and publication. Direct identifiers are minimised as far as possible.

7. Data sharing

Data is not shared with third parties for commercial purposes. Research partners may receive access to anonymised or pseudonymised data for scientific analysis.

8. Your rights

You have the right to request information about the data stored about you, as well as the right to rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, and data portability, in accordance with Articles 15–21 GDPR. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority.
